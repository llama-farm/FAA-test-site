ACS Exam Weighting — Where to Focus Your Study Time

The FAA test draws questions based on these Airman Certification Standards (ACS) content area weights:

Regulations (UA.I)
48%
Operations (UA.V)
25%
Airspace & Requirements (UA.II)
20%
Weather (UA.III)
5%
Loading & Performance (UA.IV)
2%

Strategy: Spend most time on Regulations and Operations (73% of the test). Airspace is 20% and often chart-based. Weather and Loading are lower-weight but still appear.

Getting Started — IACRA & Your FTN

Before you take the FAA knowledge test, you need an FAA Tracking Number (FTN). Get one by creating a profile in IACRA:

  1. Go to IACRA (iacra.faa.gov)
  2. Click "Register" to create a new account (or sign in if returning)
  3. Complete your profile to get your FTN
  4. Your FTN will be printed on your Airman Knowledge Test Report (AKTR) after you pass
  5. After passing the UAG test, return to IACRA to complete your Remote Pilot Certificate application
